### :technologist: About Me: 
I'm Benjamin from Germany. In 2019, I received a bachelor's degree in electrical engineering in the field of automation technology from the University of Applied Sciences in Hamburg, Germany. After graduating, I worked as an IT administrator in a small consulting company for two years. After leaving the company in early 2021, I decided to make a career change and attended a training course to become a data analyst for four months. Data modeling, including the snowflake, star, and galaxy schemes as well as the fundamentals of SQL programming, was the main focus of that course.  After I successfully completed the training, I continued learning statistics and R programming on my own. 

In 2023 I joined a medium-sized company and worked as a professional data analytics consultant, where I was involved in various client projects. I have gained extensive experience in the areas of energy supply, human resources, environment & climate, and finance, actively contributing to the analysis and optimization of various business processes. Throughout, I guided clients step by step to ensure transparency, trust, and a shared understanding. My core responsibilities included, among others:

* Development and optimization of dashboards in Tableau & Power BI to support cross-functional analyses in close collaboration with stakeholders.
* Data preparation and integration using Tableau Prep, Alteryx, and Python to ensure higher data quality and better scalability.
* Conducting workshops and training sessions as well as advising on all data- and visualization-related topics.

#### [Automated Weather Data Pipeline with Airflow and PostgreSQL](https://github.com/BenWort86/openweathermap-API)
> This project demonstrates a simple end-to-end data pipeline for collecting weather data from the OpenWeatherMap API.
> The pipeline processes both actual weather data and five-day forecasts, stores the data in a PostgreSQL database, and
> transforms it using SQL to enable comparisons between forecasted and actual weather values. Automation is handled through
> a combination of Apache Airflow for workflow orchestration and Linux cron jobs for scheduled SQL execution.

#### [Superstore Dashboard](https://github.com/BenWort86/Superstore_Dashboard)
> This small and simple dashboard was a personal project to apply the knowledge I gained while
> developing a [two-sample t-test app](https://github.com/BenWort86/Two_sample_t_test_Monte_Carlo_simulator) a few years ago.
> My main goal was to compare the process of building dashboards in Shiny versus Tableau, which I often use professionally.
> As I was only aiming to build a basic dashboard in Shiny, this project shouldn’t be seen as a demonstration of my dashboard design skills.
> That said, most of my design inspiration comes from Tableau Public. Especially the [Sample Superstore - Sales Performance-Dashboard](https://public.tableau.com/app/profile/pradeepkumar.g/viz/SampleSuperstore-SalesPerformance/viz_)
> created by Pradeep Kumar G gave the a little challenge to manage the same results (not exactly) using plotly and bslib. There are perhaps many things that could have been done better, but I'm still gaining experience in this area.
> An online version can be found [here](https://benwort-apps.shinyapps.io/Superstore_Dashboard/).
